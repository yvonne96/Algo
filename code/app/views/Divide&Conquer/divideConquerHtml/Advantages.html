Advantages of Divide & Conquer Algorithms

  • Helps to solve complex problems by breaking them down into trivial problems and combining the results.
  • Often helps in the discovery of more efficient algorithms such as quicksort and mergesort.
  • They are naturally adapted for execution in multi-processor machines since distinct sub-problems can be executed on different processors.
  • They tend to make efficient use of memory caches since that once a sub-problem is small enough, it and all its sub-problems can be solved within the cache, without accessing the slower main memory.
  • In computations with rounded arithmetic (eg.floating point numbers) they may yield more accurate results than a superficially equivalent iterative method.
